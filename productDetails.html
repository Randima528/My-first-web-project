Item Detail

Aluwa is a traditional Sri Lankan sweet treat, often enjoyed during festive occasions like Sinhala and Tamil New Year. This delicacy is made from rice flour and treacle, which is sometimes replaced with sugar. The mixture is flavored with spices such as cardamom and nutmeg, giving it a distinctive aroma and taste. Aluwa is typically shaped into diamond or rectangular pieces, boasting a crunchy texture that contrasts its sweet, spiced flavor. It's a cherished part of Sri Lanka's culinary heritage, reflecting the island's rich cultural traditions and flavors.
